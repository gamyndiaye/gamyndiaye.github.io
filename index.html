Aminata Ndiaye

Aminata Ndiaye (she/her) is a senior at Benjamin Franklin High School in New Orleans, LA and plans to pursue environmental engineering with a minor in something policy related.

Her journey with the Society of Women Engineers, FIRST Robotics, SPROUT NOLA, and K-12th students to create the Louisiana STEM leaders of tomorrow has only grown her love and passion for STEM. She has dedicated herself to STEM advocacy and for her work has been recognized as a Future Leader by I/ITSEC, a SWENext Northrop Grumman Community award winner, and a SWENext Global Innovator. She is currently the co-chair of TechNOLA, an initiative in partnership with the non-profit NOLA CODE, to provide high school students with the platform to advocate for CS education. She takes programming classes at Operation Spark to increase her computational thinking knowledge.
