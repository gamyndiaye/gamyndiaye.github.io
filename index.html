Aminata Ndiaye

I am an Environmental Engineering graduate from the University of Michigan with a deep passion for data informed solutions to environmental challenges. Growing up in New Orleans—where water shapes life and infrastructure—I became committed to addressing climate-related hazards through science, engineering, and policy.

My interests span hydrology & hydraulics, air quality, and climate resilience, and the intersection of these systems with technology and environmental justice. I believe data is one of our most powerful tools: it can inform better policy, empower communities, and help us build a smarter, more sustainable future. That is why I enjoy working with ArcGIS for spatial analysis, Python for data processing and modeling, and CAD software for infrasture design.

I love maps, sensor systems, and the storytelling potential of data. Whether through community-driven science or cutting-edge tools, my goal is to contribute to resilient systems that serve people and the planet—especially communities like mine that are often overlooked.
